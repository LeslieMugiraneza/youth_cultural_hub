Where It All Began: A Mission Rooted in Culture and Change

Our journey started with a simple observation: Rwanda's youth were becoming increasingly disconnected from their rich cultural heritage and sports traditions. Through extensive research involving over 50 youth aged 15-24, we uncovered the hidden barriers preventing young Rwandans from engaging with arts, culture, and sports.

üîç Root Causes Identified (Five Whys Analysis)

  • Poor access to public creative spaces - Limited venues for artistic expression
  • Limited community support - Lack of mentorship and guidance
  • Institutional neglect - Schools and organizations overlooking arts programs
  • Perception that arts aren't viable careers - Economic uncertainty around creative fields
  • Underrepresentation in schools - Limited curriculum focus on creative subjects

Lessons From the Field: What I Learned

üí° Key Insights

Rwanda's youth are not disinterested; they're systemically excluded. The passion exists, but the pathways don't.
Creative expression is tied to identity, healing, and opportunity. Arts and sports aren't just hobbies‚Äîthey're fundamental to youth development.
Youth need legitimacy, recognition, and tools to grow. Validation from their communities is just as important as the platforms themselves.
Tech is the bridge‚Äîbut only if paired with education and access. Digital solutions must address real-world constraints like connectivity and digital literacy.
Stakeholders must shift from tolerance to empowerment. Parents, schools, and media need to actively champion creative pursuits, not just accept them.
